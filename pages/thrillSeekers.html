Thrill Seekers

So you like adrenaline-fueled fun, huh? Disney has some great “E-Ticket rides”* that will get your heart racing. Disney parks are famous for their high energy attractions, and you might want to make your day look like this:

Thrill Seekers

Disneyland

  • Start out by getting a FASTPASS for the updated Star Tours ride; there area number of different ways this ride can now play out, so you can ride it more than once and still have a different experience. Make it your plan to hit FASTPASSes for Splash Mountain, the recently refurbished Thunder Mountain Railroad, Indiana Jones and The Temple of the Forbidden Eye, and the classic Disney roller coaster: Splash Mountain (now called "Hyperspace Mountain" in honor of Star Wars). Note that you need to return during the times shown on the FASTPASS - if you miss your window you're back in the standby line!
  • There is no FASTPASS for the Matterhorn, so get on to that bumpy old-school roller coaster whenever you like (although single rider is by far the fastest path). They put in new cars in 2013 to make it a "smoother" experience - but you'll have to decide for yourself if that's true.
  • You might want to look at Roger Rabbit’s Cartoon Spin, which often uses a FASTPASS as well. It’s not quite a thrill ride, but if you spin the wheel fast, you get a pretty fun trip through a wacky dark ride.
  • Speaking of spinning wheels, if you like to get dizzy and don’t get sick easily, take a trip on the Mad Tea Party spinning teacup ride. You get to control how fast you spin, and it can be a wild ride if you want it to be.
  • As soon as you ride most of the wild Disneyland rides, you’ll want to head over to California Adventure for its thrill attractions.

California Adventure

  • The California Adventure thrill rides you will want include Soarin’, Radiator Springs Racers, California Screamin’, Goofy’s Flight School, and Twilight Zone Tower of Terror, with all but the Tower of Terror having single rider lines, which will give you the best chance of experiencing as many of Disneyland thrills as possible in one day. If you prefer to ride in the same car as your friends, just remember you’ll be in line longer.
  • Start out by getting a FASTPASS for Soarin’ over California, as it is close to the entrance, but skip Radiator Springs Racers because even the FASTPASS line will take up half-an-hour or more.
  • If you want to get wet be sure to do Grizzly River Rapids early in the day. This ride will spin you and splash you. Be sure to put anything you don’t want to get wet (wallets, phones) in one of the free lockers by the line—and bring along a change of socks!
  • See how long the line is for single rider on Radiator Springs Racers and do single rider when it’s less than 30 minutes. The standby line is well over an hour at all times, often up to two hours on busy days. Single rider is the best option, although now that the word is out, even the single rider line can be over 30 minutes.
  • Up for some competitive fun? Head to Paradise Pier and ride Toy Story Midway Mania with its perfect blend of a spinning ride and a 3D video game.
  • For a unique thrill, try the huge Mickey’s Fun Wheel, and go for the swinging cars. It’s a nice slow ride with a great view of Disneyland, but twice each time you go around the wheel you will get a great swinging ride that is totally safe but feels terrifying to almost everyone.
  • Plan to meet up for the your mid-day check-in and don’t be late. Also, you might want to check out the Mediterranean food at the Paradise Garden Grill, or any of the other options here: http://disneyland.disney.go.com/dining/
