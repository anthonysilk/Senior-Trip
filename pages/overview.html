A Day at Disney

Welcome to Disneyland! Fans of Disney have very specific ways of touring the parks, and you can save a lot of time in line by using a little strategy (like FASTPASSes and single-rider lines).

General Advice:

  • Don't be late for your afternoon check in! Check in is between 2 and 3 pm at either: Mickey & Walt Statue in Disneyland or next to the bridge on the Pacific Wharf by Cocina Cucamonga Mexican Grill in California Adventure.
  • Make sure you have a fully charged phone and body (i.e. a good breakfast).
  • Bring your water bottle – dehydration sucks, and drinks at the park can be expensive, but there are plenty of drinking fountains.
  • Wear sunscreen. Really, you don’t want a sunburn to be your souvenir from this trip.
  • As evening starts to approach, be sure you are thinking about what final things you want to do or what shops you want to visit. Be on time for the evening check in at 8 pm in the Esplanade between the parks.
  • If you want to have a nice lunch at the park, there are a number of sit-down restaurants, but you should make reservations in advance (note: these restaurants can be expensive). Blue Bayou inside the Pirates of the Caribbean is a favorite for many people because of the ambiance. To make a reservation call Disney dining: (714) 781-7290.
  • Get a park map and entertainment guide for both parks. Not only is it useful to know when the parades are so you can attend one, but you also will know where everyone will be so that you will have shorter lines for rides during peak show times.

Remember, there are as many ways to enjoy the parks as there are visitors. There will be far more to do than you will have time in one day if you try to see all that the parks have to offer.

  • If you need more advice, there are number of apps available for iPhones and Androids as well as the Disneyland website: http://disneyland.disneyland.go.com
  • Also, check out the Disneyland Wait Time apps, which will give you constant data on how long every line is.
