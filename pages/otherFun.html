All Kinds of Fun

Other Stuff To Do

Disneyland

  • If you make a list of a few of the main E-ticket attractions (see the note in A Day at Disney For Thrillseekers) you want to hit, you can use your extra time to ride some of the more mellow rides in the parks. If you want to have a song stuck in your head all day, take a trip on It’s a Small World, see the pirates in Pirates of the Caribbean, and the ghosts in the Haunted Mansion.
  • Feeling competitive? Bring a friend on Disneyland’s Buzz Lightyear Astro Blasters and see who can score the most points as you destroy the evil Zurg’s space minions! Compare it to California Adventure’s Toy Story Midway Mania and see who can score the most points in the most amazing digital arcade games you’ll ever ride!
  • If the kid in you wants to enjoy some fun dark rides of Disney’s best stories, Peter Pan’s Flight is always a favorite, as you soar over London and Neverland. Then you can ride Mr. Toad’s Wild Ride, Snow White’s Scary Adventure, Pinocchio's Daring Journey, and Alice in Wonderland. Finding Nemo Submarine Voyage is a reworking of the old 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea ride, and it is a novel way to spend 10 minutes with Nemo and friends. The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh is another dark ride, on a cute little beehive that rocks slowly as you ride through the story of his blustery day, full of heffalumps and woozles…and Tigger too.
  • Tomorrowland’s Autopia is an easy little trip via a miniature freeway that weaves through the back of Tomorrowland.
  • Star Tours is a bit more bumpy, but it’s recent refresh makes it a fun 3D ride through a galaxy far, far away. A FASTPASS for this one is recommended.
  • And if you just want to see someone smile, buy a balloon and give it to a random child (say it's a gift from Mickey).

California Adventure

  • A fun but easy day in California Adventure starts with a trip down Buena Vista Street and the sounds, sights, and textures of Los Angeles as Walt Disney saw it when he arrived in 1920s Hollywood. Next pick up a FASTPASS for Soarin’ over California, if you are not afraid of heights. This ride is hard to describe, but it is a favorite of many and can really be a great break on a hot day.
  • You might want to ride the Red Car Trolley to Hollywood Land, passing the Carthay Circle Theater next to the central fountain. In Hollywood Land, you can ride another dark ride: Monsters, Inc. Mike and Sully to the Rescue.
  • A Bug’s Land has a number of slower, well-themed rides based on Pixar’s A Bug’s Life. This is a great place to spend a little time, especially at dusk as the lighting in this area is beautiful at night.
  • Moving clockwise in the park, you’ll come to the brand new Cars Land, and if you skip the long lines of Radiator Springs Racers, you can enjoy the tastes, sights, and sounds of Pixar’s Cars. Take a ride on the brand new Luigi's Rollickin' Roadsters or Mater's Junkyard Jamboree for some low-impact silliness.
  • Continuing clockwise, you’ll pass through the Pacific Wharf area, which is a fantastic place to enjoy lunch, with many great options and flavors to choose from.
  • Moving past the wharf, you will come upon a Victorian seaside park called Paradise Pier. Ride the fixed cars on Mickey’s Fun Wheel for a great view of Disneyland a rest, and a nice breeze. The other rides in this area tend to be very low-speed experiences, but be sure to take a look at: The Little Mermaid: Ariel's Undersea Adventure, especially if you are fan of the movie. And don't miss Toy Story Midway Mania, which is great fun if you love video games and/or are the competitive type.
