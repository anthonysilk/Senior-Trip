Taking it Slow

If you don’t feel like standing in line at all and if you want to be entertained by shows (both live and animatronic), Disneyland and Disney California Adventure have much to offer you! You will want to make note of the start times of shows (get a guide when you enter each park) and give yourself time for walking. In addition, be sure to look at the slower dark rides discussed in the All Kinds of Fun plan to fill out a leisurely day in the parks.

Taking it Slow

Disneyland

  • If you are looking for a quiet spot to rest, The Court des Anges in New Orleans Square not only provides a peaceful respite, but will transport you to another time and place. You may also want to try the area by Harbour Galley and Fowler’s Inn which offers a riverfront landscape with the gentle sound of lapping water.
  • Is it hot out? Try the Pirates of the Caribbean ride. Although it has a few drops, for the most part it's a leisurely ride in a boat. Like the boat, but not the drops? Head to It's a Small World, for a quiet ride and lots of cheerful music!
  • The animatronic shows are also fun and easy to hit if the timing is right as you pass by. The Enchanted Tiki Room is a silly, but enjoyable show (get a Dole Whip while you wait!) and Mr. Lincoln speaks to audiences throughout the day on Main Street in Great Moments with Mr. Lincoln.
  • You can walk through the famous castle and re-live Sleeping Beauty’s adventures, and you can also walk through Tarzan’s Treehouse in Adventureland.
  • Want to spend the day relaxing with a cup of coffee or over a good meal? Try the Café Orleans.
  • Remember to check out the shops, or just sit on a few benches and let the sounds, sights, smells, and tastes of the park transport you to different times and places as you move from one themed land to another.

California Adventure

  • Silly Symphony Swings at Disney California Adventure park has a beautiful covered promenade. While guests are swinging high above, you can take in the stunning views of Paradise Pier, The Little Mermaid ~ Ariel’s Undersea Adventure and The Golden Vine Winery across Paradise Bay.
  • Also at Disney California Adventure park, Grizzly Trail provides a serene nature walk past Grizzly Falls on the way to Paradise Pier or Pacific Wharf.
  • The parades, the Red Car Trolley boys, and the other live performers are brief but fully professionally staged shows that occur throughout the parks. Or wonder down Buena Vista street to meet and talk to some of the wacky characters hanging about.
  • For food and drink there are lots of places in Pacific Wharf, but for a little more quiet, head up to the Paradise Garden Grill.
  • Another great respite is the lobby of the Grand Californian Hotel. The couches near the fireplace are an especially good place to read or people watch. There is an entrance to the hotel from inside CA, but note you may have to re-enter the parks through one of the main gates.

Downtown Disney

  • If you are truly not a fan of the parks, you can always spend the day strolling through Downtown Disney, an outdoor mall full of shops, kiosks, and restaurants. Most of the shops are non-Disney, but if you are looking for some Disney souvenirs you will find the largest Disney Store out here too. And while you’re out and about be sure to check the amazing Disney inspired Lego creations at the Lego store. From Downtown Disney you can either walk back to the main gates, or take the monorail directly to Tomorrowland.
